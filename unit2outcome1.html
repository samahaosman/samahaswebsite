1.2 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Copy right refers to the legal protection of one’s original work of creation. This can be written, film, artistic or other forms of creative expressions. Websites are strongly categorised as copyrightable materials, due to the inclusion of written materials, graphics, logos, and other visual and sound elements. The UK’s Copyright and patent act of 1998 gives creators control of they would like their material to be used. Websites often implement copyright notices to the footer to remind users of this protection [18].

In web design many projects suffer from constraints, such as technology, budget, and deadline etc, which can have a significant impact in designing a website. Although these constraints may restrict your freedom, through taking away impactful options, they can encourage more creative thinking [19].

1.4 Describe which are the appropriate filetypes for websites

When creating a website there are so many different file types to choose from but the four most suitable file formats are JPEG, GIF, SVG and PNG. These file formats contain lossy or lossless compressions. A lossy compression means that when the file size decreases that image loses information and quality. But a lossless compression does not lose any information when the file size changes

Choosing the right file format is important as it can affect the way images are presented on your website and user may not be able to see it. Also, incorrect file format can make your website slow [21].
